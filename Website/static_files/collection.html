Game Timeline Overview

Excluding the warmup, the game will last anywhere from 1 to 1 1⁄2 hours. The beginning of the game is expected to be slow, with players figuring out initial roles and deciding on a plan of action. A player will probably start planting seeds, another will explore the home area, and another will venture out to get a feel for the maze. Although the players will be briefed on everything during the warmup, the game is still expected to be foreign until they start getting their hands dirty.

After a brief exploration, players will settle into a routine style of gameplay. Whatever players are allocated to be maze explorers will explore the maze, and whatever players are allocated to be resource collectors will collect resources from the home area. Once nightfall hits, players will either group up and sleep or group up in the home area to avoid dangerous night mobs.

About an hour into gameplay, the players should have reached a point where some or all of them can survive nights in the maze. At this point, it may be advantageous to leave the center area and only return to stock up on food.

There will not be any point where the game is “finished”. Once the duration we decide on has passed, the game will be over. The score of the team will be based on the number of treasures they collected.


HeapCraft

HeapCraft is a project designed and created as a multi-disciplinary project to measure, analyze, and improve collaboration and cooperation in Minecraft. This data from a virtual world could then be taken and applied to real life in certain scenarios.


Epilog

This is the main tool under HeapCraft, it is the primary way that data is collected. It intercepts, classifies, and logs all the player behavior events that happen on any server using it as a plugin. Epilog is the main part of the HeapCraft project and the basis for most of the work that we are going to be doing in our project.
